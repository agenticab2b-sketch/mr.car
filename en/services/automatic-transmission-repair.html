Specialized automatic transmission diagnostics and repair in Tallinn

The automatic transmission is one of the most complex components of a modern car. It contains hundreds of hydraulic, mechanical, and electronic components working with high precision. A failure in one part can quickly damage the entire system.

Mr.Car specializes in the diagnostics and repair of automatic transmissions. We use specialized equipment to pinpoint the exact cause of the failure and prevent expensive complete replacements. We repair classic automatic transmissions, as well as DSG and ZF gearboxes.

Why shouldn't you delay fixing automatic transmission problems?
An automatic transmission problem won't fix itself — it gets worse with every kilometre. Metal debris spreads through the system, clogging valve body channels and wearing down hydraulic parts. The sooner you come in, the smaller and cheaper the repair. Wait too long and you're looking at a full unit replacement.

How to spot an automatic transmission failure?

Main symptoms that should not be ignored

Jerking and harsh shifting

Harsh kicks when shifting indicate valve body wear, low oil pressure, or a faulty torque converter in the transmission.

READ WHY THE TRANSMISSION JERKS →

Slipping gears

An increase in RPM without corresponding acceleration means the clutch packs are no longer holding gear and torque is lost.

READ WHAT SLIPPING MEANS →

Delayed shifting

The transmission shifts into gear with a noticeable delay — caused by low pressure, a clogged valve body, or a mechatronics issue.

READ ABOUT SHIFT DELAYS →

Noise and whining

Whining, rustling, or clunking sounds indicate wear in bearings, gears, or the torque converter.

READ ABOUT TRANSMISSION NOISES →

Fluid leaks

Reddish-brown spots under the car → wear on pressure seals. A drop in fluid level quickly leads to severe and expensive damage.

READ ABOUT STOPPING FLUID LEAKS →

Limp mode

The car switches to a safety mode and displays an error — a protective function that prevents the transmission from seizing. Immediate diagnostics are required.

READ WHAT TO DO IN LIMP MODE →

What happens if you ignore transmission faults?

1
Early symptoms

Slight delays, gentle jerking — diagnostics identify the problem at an early stage. Often, a fluid change or valve body cleaning is enough.

2
Damage worsens

The transmission slips, limp mode activates. Metal shavings spread. The valve body and torque converter will need repair.

3
Complete failure

The transmission refuses to shift at all. Full disassembly, rebuild, or replacement required. The most expensive outcome — and entirely avoidable with early action.

Transmission diagnostics — the only correct first step

A successful transmission repair always begins with precise diagnostics. Blindly replacing parts wastes your budget and might not solve the issue.

  • Computer diagnostics — reading fault codes and live data
  • Hydraulic pressure testing — checking valve functionality
  • Fluid analysis — identifying metal shavings and wear

Learn more about the diagnostics process →

Main automatic transmission repair solutions

Depending on the diagnostics results, we choose the best path

1. Fluid & Filter change

Dynamic fluid flush and change. Removes wear debris from the old fluid and solves light jerking at an early stage.

2. Mechatronics repair

Valve body flushing, replacing defective solenoids and seals. Re-establishes fast gear shifts and eliminates delays or pressure drops.

3. Complete rebuild

Transmission removal and disassembly. Friction discs, seals, and damaged parts are replaced. Necessary if the mechanism has physically failed.

DSG and ZF transmissions — high-tech solutions

DSG (Direct Shift Gearbox) is a dual-clutch transmission developed by the Volkswagen Group, found in VW, Audi, Škoda, and SEAT vehicles. It is sensitive to fluid condition and mechatronics issues. Mr.Car repairs DSG gearboxes using specialized equipment.

ZF produces widely used automatic transmissions (8HP, 9HP) installed in premium vehicles like BMW, Volvo, and Land Rover. ZF transmissions require specific diagnostic tools and special transmission fluid. Mr.Car fully diagnoses and repairs ZF gearboxes.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between a classic automatic and a DSG?
A classic automatic uses a torque converter that ensures a very smooth ride. A DSG is a dual-clutch transmission that shifts much faster and is more efficient, but has a different build and requires specific maintenance.
What is the difference between an automatic and CVT?
A CVT resembles an automatic transmission when driving, but it lacks stepped gears. Mr.Car specializes only in stepped automatics (including DSG and ZF). We currently do not repair CVT-type transmissions.
How much does it cost to repair an automatic transmission?
Because this transmission is highly complex, the exact repair cost is only revealed after diagnostics. Computer scanning and pressure measurements give a clear picture of what needs rebuilding or replacing. We provide a clear quotation before starting.
Can I drive an automatic transmission in limp mode?
Limp mode is a protective function — not a sign that it's safe to keep driving. You should not drive in limp mode. Get the car to a workshop for diagnostics immediately.
How often should transmission fluid be changed?
A transmission fluid and filter change is highly recommended every 60,000 – 80,000 km to guarantee a long lifespan and smooth operation.
What happens if I delay fluid change too long?
Old fluid loses its lubricating properties and collects metallic dust from worn parts. This debris acts as an abrasive, damaging seals, valves, and clogging filters, eventually leading to pressure drops and complete failure.
Suspecting an automatic transmission issue?
Don't wait until it's completely seized. A diagnostic check pinpoints the problem and saves you money on the repair.
